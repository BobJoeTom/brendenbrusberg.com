    1. Defining

 

  • Programming Skills

 

        1. Programming Skills Challenge Rules:
          1. 60 Seconds Long - 100% Autonomous
          2. 3 Stars and 2 cubes can be manually loaded onto the starting tile or the robot at any point
          3. One preload star may be placed touching the robot as long as it doesn’t touch any grey tiles and is fully inside the field perimeter
          4. Before starting, the bot may not touch any tiles except for the starting tile and it may not touch any scoring objects except for the one preloaded star
          5. Low hanging and high hanging available
            1. Low hanging is completely off the ground and hanging from the pvc pipe, whereas high hanging is completely above the plane formed by the top of the perimeter wall
        2. Programming Skills Scoring:
          1. Star Near zone - 1
          2. Star Far zone - 2
          3. Cube Near - 2
          4. Cube Far - 4
          5. Low Hang - 4
          6. High Hang - 12

 

  • The Match

 

        1. Autonomous Segment
          1. 15 seconds long
          2. Scoring (by points)
            1. Star in Near zone - 1
            2. Star in Far zone - 2
            3. Cube in Near zone - 2
            4. Cube in Far zone - 4
          3. One star may be preloaded before this segment. The robot must be in one of the starting squares and must not be touching any other square or object that is not the preloaded star. The robot and star must be fully inside of the starting area.
          4. Robot must work without the assistance of the team, and can only be worked on if the robot does not function properly.
        2. Driver Control Segment
          1. 1 minute and 45 seconds long
          2. Scoring  
            1. Star in Near zone - 1
            2. Star in Far zone - 2
            3. Cube in Near zone - 2
            4. Cube in Far zone - 4
          3. There is one cube preload allowed in this segment of the competition.
          4. The robot can only be manually driven by one team member. It can only be worked with if it is not functioning properly.

 

  • Robot Skills Challenge

 

        1. Robot Skills Challenge Rules:
          1. The robot must not be touching any tile other than the alliance starting tile and may only touch the one preloaded star
          2. The preload start may not touch any grey foam tiles and must be in contact with the robot at the start of the challenge
          3. Robot skills loads may be loaded at any point in the match as long as they are loaded onto the alliance starting tile or into the robot
          4. 60 second driver control period
          5. The skills loads consist of 3 stars and 2 cubes
        2. Robot Skills Challenge Scoring:
          1. *Same scoring as programming skills*

 

  • Robot Rules

 

      1. One robot allowed to compete per team
      2. Subsystems:
        1. Mobile robotic base (Navigation) - The base of a stationary robot will be considered subsystem 1
        2. Power and control system
        3. Additional mechanisms that allow for manipulation of game objects or navigation of field obstacles
      3. Any mechanisms or components that could potentially damage the playing field, damage another robot, or get easily tangled with another robot will be disqualified until it passes re-inspection.
      4. Size limit: 18’’ x 18’’ x 18’’ at start of match
      5. Robots may expand beyond their starting size constraints after the start of a match
      6. Robot must be re-inspected after major changes
      7. Lubrication is allowed in any areas of the robot that will not touch the playing field
      8. If using a speaker, any music must be “in good taste”. Highly important.
      9. Tape may be used for labeling, securing wires, preventing leaks of pneumatic fittings, and securing a VEXnet key to the microcontroller.
      10. Hot glue may be used for cable connections
      11. We may use an unlimited amount of â…›’’ braided nylon rope
      12. Robot must use only one VEX EDR microcontroller
      13. Robots can use either 10 motors and a legal vrc pneumatic system OR 12 motors.
      14. The robot may use one 7.2V battery unless a power expander is used. With a power expander, a 9V backup battery is required and an additional 7.2V battery is allowed
      15. Pneumatic devices may be charged to a max of 100 psi

 

  1. Research
    1. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1m_MSoY8g3c#t=2.956732 - This video showcased a robot that was able to pick up many game objects at a time and was mainly inhibited by its movement and rotation speed.  One thing this robot credited its efficiency to was the fact that it could drive forward (away from the center wall), pick up game objects, and then throw them backwards over the wall.  This reduced the amount turning required to score points.
    2. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MCj7G-5FGXk - This video perfectly displays numerous design flaws that appear often during that matches that we have seen.  The two robots in the foreground of the video struggle such that they can only pick up one star at once, and they do so very slowly due to poorly designed collection mechanisms.  Also, the two robots in the background tipped over multiple times since the launching mechanisms were so heavy compared to the narrow base.  
    3. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FCck9_vk8H4 - In this competition, apart from the autonomous segment, the team on the right excelled throughout the majority of the match.  The robot with the scissor lift on that alliance was very efficient as it was not only able to pick up 3 stars at once, but it was also able to utilize its height to block the opponent’s stars.  The robot utilizing a forklift to pick up the stars and cubes was also extremely efficient such that its “arm” pivoted at the top of the robot which allowed it to block the opponent as well.  In addition, its arm functioned very quickly as there were a total of 6 motors being used to power the launching mechanism.  
    4. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-y7MuLl_cyw - This video showcases a lift mechanism that would be very compatible with the design of our robots throwing mechanism.  The lift utilizes the same motors that raise the arm to provide the driving force required to lift the rest of the robot off of the ground.  The only difference there would be in terms of applying this type of design to our robot would be that our robot faces the opposite direction, but the concept shown in this video is still applicable.
